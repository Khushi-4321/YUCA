The Global Challenge of Zero Hunger

Achieving Zero Hunger is a critical goal for sustainable development. Millions around the world face food insecurity, and the challenge is exacerbated by climate change, resource scarcity, and inefficient food systems. YUCA aims to provide resources and solutions to tackle these issues head-on.

Food waste is a significant contributor to this problem. According to the FAO, roughly one-third of all food produced for human consumption is wasted or lost. This waste not only deprives people of essential nutrients but also contributes to environmental degradation. YUCA promotes strategies to reduce food waste at every stage, from farm to table.

Sustainable agriculture is another key focus. By encouraging practices like crop rotation, reduced pesticide use, and efficient water management, we can ensure long-term food security while minimizing environmental impact.

Stakeholder Suggestions

Students

  • Reduce food waste in school canteens.
  • Learn about sustainable agriculture.
  • Participate in community gardens.
  • Organize food drives and awareness campaigns.

Farmers

  • Adopt efficient irrigation techniques.
  • Use organic fertilizers and integrated pest management.
  • Improve post-harvest storage and transportation.
  • Diversify crops to build resilience.

Policy Makers

  • Implement food waste reduction policies.
  • Support sustainable farming practices through subsidies and incentives.
  • Invest in food security programs and infrastructure.
  • Promote public-private partnerships for food security.
